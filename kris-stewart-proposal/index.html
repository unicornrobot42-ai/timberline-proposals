0

Plans, Architecture & Permitting

Start immediately · Foundation for everything
✅ Start Now

Before any construction begins, we need a full set of architectural drawings — both as-is (the house as it exists today) and projected (the house as we intend to build it). These drawings are done by a licensed architect and structural engineer and become the legal backbone of the entire project.

Once drawings are complete, we submit to the city for permit approval. Timberline manages the entire submittal process — fielding city comments, making required revisions, and driving the plans to a final approved set. This phase is what gives us an accurate, fixed price for Phases 1–3.

Why This Comes First

Without approved plans, any budget is an estimate. With approved plans, we can give you a fixed-price contract. Phase 0 is the single most important investment in making this project run smoothly and on budget.

Living Room Addition

Front of house · 6–8 ft extension

We'll extend the front of the home by 6 to 8 feet, adding meaningful square footage to the living area. This is a full structural addition — new foundation, framing, roofing tie-in, exterior finish to match the existing home, windows, electrical, insulation, drywall, and interior paint.

The exact scope and cost will be finalized once architectural drawings define the footprint and any structural requirements. The range below reflects typical OC market costs for this type of addition.

2

Kitchen Remodel + Whole-Home Flooring

Full kitchen redesign · 1,665 sq ft new floors

A complete kitchen transformation: wall removal to open up the layout, all-new cabinetry, countertops, backsplash, plumbing, and electrical. Appliances are not included in this scope (client to purchase separately or we can coordinate install only).

Simultaneously, we'll remove and replace all flooring throughout the entire home — approximately 1,665 square feet — including new baseboard and stair treads. New flooring ties the updated spaces together and sets the finish level for the entire house.

Upstairs Addition

2nd story expansion · Master bedroom + new bathroom

The most complex phase: a second-story addition that extends the master bedroom and adds a full bathroom. This requires structural engineering to ensure the existing first floor can support the new load, new framing, roofing tie-in, exterior finish, full bathroom build-out (tile, plumbing, fixtures, glass enclosure), electrical, drywall, insulation, flooring, and all interior finishes.

The wide range below reflects the significant variables in second-story addition work — the exact square footage added, bathroom finish level, and structural requirements will all be determined by the plans. This is the number we expect to tighten most significantly after Phase 0 is complete.
